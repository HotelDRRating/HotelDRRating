FREQUENTLY ASKED QUESTIONS

What is the Disaster Resilience Rating for Hotels? The DRRH is a thesis (capstone) project by a group of graduate students from the Asian Institute of Management (AIM), centered on co-creating a disaster resilience rating together with hotel industry associations/groups that will be shared publicly via Online Travel Agents (OTAs) and other media platforms as a way of gaining confidence from the general public.
How will the rating measure the hotel's level of disaster resilience? The DRRH follows the Resilience Programming Framework (RPF) created by Resilient.PH. The rating will evaluate the management practices â€” Asset Protection and Risk Management (APRM), Business Continuity Management (BCM), Disaster Risk Reduction (DRR), Emergency, Crisis and Opportunity Management (ECOM) and Risk/Crisis Communication (R/CCOMM) by reviewing existing policies, programs, systems, trainings, and tools (including finances) of hotels and assigning scores based on resilient level criteria - base (for local industry practices/compliance driven practices) mid (international best practice/above compliance) and high (for proven effective novel practices/thought leadership).
How will the hotel benefit from the rating? With the DRRH, hotels will be able to identify vulnerable components of their operations which they can work on to build their overall resilience.
More importantly, the rating will show the hotelâ€™s commitment to ensuring safety and wellbeing of guests and employees before, during, and after any hazard event which will add to the positive perception/image of the hotel.
The survey we conducted among hotel goers show that guests are likely to choose a hotel that they perceive to be resilient.
