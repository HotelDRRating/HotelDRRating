FREQUENTLY ASKED QUESTIONS

What is the Disaster Resilience Rating for Hotels?

The DRRH is a thesis (capstone) project by a group of graduate students from the Asian Institute of Management (AIM), centered on co-creating a disaster resilience rating together with hotel industry associations/groups that will be shared publicly via Online Travel Agents (OTAs) and other media platforms as a way of gaining confidence from the general public.

How will the rating measure the hotel's level of disaster resilience?

The DRRH follows the Resilience Programming Framework (RPF) created by Resilient.PH. The rating will evaluate the management practices — Asset Protection and Risk Management (APRM), Business Continuity Management (BCM), Disaster Risk Reduction (DRR), Emergency, Crisis and Opportunity Management (ECOM) and Risk/Crisis Communication (R/CCOMM) by reviewing existing policies, programs, systems, trainings, and tools (including finances) of hotels and assigning scores based on resilient level criteria - base (for local industry practices/compliance driven practices) mid (international best practice/above compliance) and high (for proven effective novel practices/thought leadership).

How will the hotel benefit from the rating?

With the DRRH, hotels will be able to identify vulnerable components of their operations which they can work on to build their overall resilience.

More importantly, the rating will show the hotel’s commitment to ensuring safety and wellbeing of guests and employees before, during, and after any hazard event which will add to the positive perception/image of the hotel.

The survey we conducted among hotel goers show that guests are likely to choose a hotel that they perceive to be resilient.

How much will the rating cost?

The cost that will be charged to the hotel is the conduct of the third-party audit.

Initial estimates show that this may run anywhere between Php50 - 75K excluding travel and accommodation expenses for field audit.

What is the process for getting a hotel rated?

Subject to the directions of the hotel industry association, the DRRH may be available to both members and non-members.

Hotels who would like to get rated simply has to register via website or app (or via online forms).

What are the procedures in having a hotel rated?

After registration, the hotel will be guided on the process of self-assessment, the natural starting point of the evaluation.

Once the self-assessment is completed and the documentary requirements have been submitted, a third party auditor will coordinate with the hotel for the actual field audit where evaluators will visit the hotel and validate the self-assessment.

The auditor will then submit the findings to the industry association who will serve as the final approver of the rating.

Once approved, the hotel will be allowed to use the rating in all its promotional activities.

What does the hotel need to provide/submit to be rated?

As the critical areas for review includes policies, programs, systems, training, and tools (including finances), the rating will require hotels to show proof of the elements of the RPF in these areas.

Description of training programs for safety, company policy on disaster risk reduction, photos of health and safety features and resources, etc. are some of the examples of documents that will be asked of hotels undergoing the rating.

What will the auditors/inspectors do when conducting the rating?

The third-party audit process has two components — the first part includes checking the completeness and validity of documents submitted by the hotel being audited. The self-assessment results will be reviewed following the criteria set by the DRRH.

The second part involves field auditors visiting the hotel and validating the documents in the self-assessment through actual inspection, interview with employees and guests, interview with local government agencies and the community.

Can the hotel suddenly back-out during the conduct of the rating? Will the payment be refunded?

The rating will only commence after payment of audit fees and completion of the self-assessment.

There is a time for completing the self-assessment as well as time period for the field audit and the final approval.

Should a hotel wish to discontinue the rating process, after the self-assessment, it will have to formally advise the industry association, but the fee will not be refunded when the field audit has been conducted.

How long is the whole rating process?

The self-assessment is to be completed within one month. The first part of the third-party audit will begin as soon as the self-assessment has been submitted and will run for a maximum of one week.

The field audit will be done after the desk review has been completed and is expected to last anywhere between 3 to 5 days. The findings of the third-party audit will be transmitted to the hotel industry association who will be given a maximum of two weeks to approve or reject the rating.

If there are no questions about the audit findings, the final approval will be communicated two weeks after submission of the third-party audit findings.

What will happen after the field audit?

The findings of the third-party audit will be sent to the hotel industry association for final approval.

Once approved, the hotel industry association will send out a certificate of rating approval which the hotel can share with their respective OTAs as proof of authenticity of the rating.

The same certification can also be used by the hotel in all their promotion and marketing activities.

Hotels that will receive a High (RRR) rating will be awarded the certificate via a live public event at their venue of choice.

Who will evaluate and approve the rating?

The self-assessment will have to be cleared and approved by the management of the hotel.

The third-party audit results that is based on the self-assessment will be evaluated by the hotel industry association who will also act as the final approving authority.

Who will publish the rating?

The hotel can share their industry approved rating with their partner OTAs as well as with their other media partners.

The hotel industry association will also maintain a list of all rated hotels in its website.

Where will the rating of the hotel be published?

The hotel can choose where their approved rating will be published.

How will the rating be published?

OTAs can develop their own process for publishing the ratings as well as other media entities that hotel chooses to share their approved rating with.

But the industry will also maintain a section of their website (or an independent website) where all hotels rated will be featured.

How long is the validity of the rating?

The rating will be valid for 1 year after the formal transmission of the certificate of rating approval from the industry association.

This is subject for change based on the auditing body. If there is any contest on the rating, the hotel industry (HRAP) will be the one to decide on the final rating.

How often does a hotel need to be rated?

The DRRH will be conducted annually.

But succeeding audits are expected to be completed within a shorter time frame as the hotel has already submitted substantial documentation.

What will happen to the published rating if the hotel is suddenly affected by a disaster?

The DRRH is an evaluation of the existing resilience measures of a hotel.

Should a disaster hits, the industry association may decide to check whether these measures were used or not or whether these measures were effective or not.

The rating will not be changed until after the next audit review.
